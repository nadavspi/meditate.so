    A Crash Course in Meditation

  • Meditation comprises a variety of techniques that target
    Meditation comprises a variety of techniques that target the functioning of the mind. It can lead to profound perceptual and cognitive transformations that have traditonally been called spiritual enlightenment or awakening.

    Turtle: It might be more accurate to say *enlightenments* or *awaken-ing*, since the results of meditation are neither uniform nor steady state.

  • Meditation can make you feel good, but it can also
    Meditation can make you feel good, but it can also be boring, stressful, and frustrating at times.

    Turtle: It’s not a linear development of feeling better and better.

  • Not about stopping thoughts
    Meditation is not about stopping your thoughts. Success is not determined by how few thoughts you have.

    Turtle: You might be shocked by how many thoughts you notice as soon as you sit down to meditate. That’s okay!

  • Vipassana
    There are many kinds of meditation techniques that produce myriad results.
    We’re going to focus on *vipassana* or *insight meditation*, which is the process of continually noticing your experience and the sensations that it comprises.

    Turtle: The kind of vipassana we’re talking about has its roots in Theravada Buddhism.

  • Buddhism
    Although meditation comes from Buddhism, it has continued to evolve since coming to the West. As meditation has made its way into schools, hospitals, and workplaces, many teachers have developed a secular approach to these ancient practices.

    Shoshana Cooper quote

    Turtle: It’s possible to divorce meditation from the religious trappings of its history, and look at it as as a technology for living a better life.

  • Noting
    Earlier we said that vipassana is the process of continually noticing your experience and the sensations that it comprises. One way to do this is with the *noting technique*, where you use one word labels to describe whatever you’re experiencing. Every few seconds, you name whatever is taking center stage in that moment and note it silently or aloud.
